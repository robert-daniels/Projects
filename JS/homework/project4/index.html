Hello !

  1. The alert(), confirm(), and prompt() methods are methods of which object?

    They are methods of the window object.

  2. Why can we use the alert(), confirm(), and prompt() methods without preceding them with the object. (object dot) syntax?

    Window is a global object which is already provided upon build. One can technically use window.alert() if for some reason they like typing more.

  3. How do we distinguish variable names from strings in JavaScript?

    Strings are enclosed in quotation marks. Variables are simply typed out rote.

  4. ...What's the point of learning about these annoying popup boxes?

    The popup boxes are ready "off the shelf." They are provided to us by the Window object. Users are familiar with them, and typically know how to interact with them. In other words, sometimes you don't have to re-invent the wheel. Why would you go out and sharpen a stick when you have a knife readily available?
